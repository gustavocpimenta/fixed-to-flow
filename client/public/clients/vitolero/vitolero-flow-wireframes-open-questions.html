Q1
Navigation Model: Tab Bar Hidden in Snap Mode Resolved for MVP
The approach: 4-tab bar (Home, Snap, Journal, Profile) visible on all screens except camera. Snap mode goes full-screen with an X to exit. This preserves the immersive camera feel while giving persistent access to Journal and the rest of the app.
Resolved May 2026 · MVP answer: two-pip chrome instead of a tab bar. Camera pip (bottom-right) on every non-camera screen except those where the camera is the implicit next action (sommelier, cigar card, filter preview, share). Journal pip (top-right) is hidden on the very first session and unlocks when the first identification auto-saves — Seek-style progressive reveal. Account creation stays deferred to the first Journal tap (PRD §6.3). See the main wireframes page for the applied rules and the navigation-plan.md for the full pattern catalog.
Post April 17 update (preserved for Phase 2): Home and Profile tabs are not part of the MVP — both depend on the recommendations engine and taste profile (post-MVP). The four-tab model below is preserved as the Phase 2 navigation target once Home and Profile have content to show.

Q2

Current coverage: Flow 1c (Photo Import) and the text/voice search ideas below are all post-MVP per PRD §5. Photo import is blocked by Meta API restrictions for FB and queued for after MVP for camera-roll. Text/voice search has no MVP correlate — the MVP is built around the photo-first hero loop.
Post April 17 update: the MVP answer for "no cigar at hand" is intentionally minimal — come back when you have one. Q3
Mode Switching: Quick to Reflective and Back Resolved for MVP Phase 2 reference
The problem: Dave snaps a cigar (Quick), then wants to check his diary from last week (Reflective), then snaps a friend's cigar (Quick again). These are not separate sessions. How does switching work?
Resolved May 2026 · MVP answer (Option B won): the floating camera pip (bottom-right) is the universal way back to camera. It appears on every screen except the camera itself and screens where camera is the implicit next action. From Journal, Pairing, or the Returning User last-photo state, one tap returns Dave to the viewfinder. No tab bar, no swipe gesture — just a persistent corner pip. See the Returning User flow and navigation-plan.md.

Q4
Cigar Detail: One Component, Three Contexts MVP-relevant
The problem: The cigar detail appears in three different flows. The information layer is always the same (identity, flavor, strength, rating). But the actions change depending on context: did you just snap it, are you browsing a recommendation, or are you reviewing a past smoke? And Pairing (a core feature) should be accessible from all of them.
Post April 17 update: the MVP has two contexts, not three — After Snap (action bar: Share primary, Pair, Save) and From Journal (action bar: Share, Pair, Heart). The "From Recommendation" context ships with Phase 2. Note also: brand and line only at MVP — vitola, wrapper, binder, filler, ring gauge, smoke time, and price range (Tier 3) are deferred. The Tier 3 expanded detail frame below is Phase 2 reference.
Information tiers (same in all contexts):
Tier 1: Identity (always visible)
Photo (user's snap or database image) / Brand + Line (e.g. "Padron 1964 Anniversary Series") / Vitola + wrapper (e.g. "Imperial, Natural") / Origin flag
Tier 2: Character (always visible, persona-adapted)
Flavor notes with icons (Cocoa, Coffee, Cream, Chocolate, Earth) / Strength gauge / Community rating (stars) / Body descriptor ("Medium-full, rich and complex")
Tier 3: Deep Detail (expand/scroll, enthusiast+)
Flavor by thirds (first, middle, final) / Wrapper, binder, filler leaf breakdown / Ring gauge + length / Smoke time estimate / Price range / Production details, aging notes
Three contexts, three action bars:
Context Scenario Actions (concept.md source)
After Snap Just identified a cigar (State 3) Save to diary, Share, Find similar, Pair, Expand detail
From Recommendation Tapped a ranked card at the shop (State 4, Flow 2 Step 5) Pair, Share, Find similar. User is deciding whether to buy.
From Diary Tapped a past smoke entry (Section 5.7) Share, Pair, Find similar. Reviewing what you smoked.
Key insight: Pair appears in all three contexts. It is one of the 5 core features (Section 5.5). "I have a cigar, suggest a drink" is a natural action from any cigar detail. The concept defines pairing as a unified feature regardless of direction.

Open design questions:
Q4a. Pair from everywhere? The concept defines pairing as a core feature (Section 5.5). Should "Pair this" be available from every cigar detail, or only when the user is actively with the cigar (After Snap, not Diary)?
Q4b. Rating: when does it happen? The concept says rating is "optional" and part of minimum snap data (Section 5.1). But can you rate a cigar later from the diary, or only at snap time? The concept says active input "exists for enthusiasts but is never required" (Principle 3), but does not specify when.
Q4c. Share from diary? The concept defines the share card as generated after a snap (Section 5.1 step 7, State 9). Can you generate a share card for a cigar you smoked last week? The photo and data exist. This would make the diary a second distribution channel.
