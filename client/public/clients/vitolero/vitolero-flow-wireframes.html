v2.1 · MVP only · Post April 17 pivot · April 2026 · Fixed to Flow

April 17, 2026 · Snap & Share Pivot

The product is now a 3-feature MVP: Snap & Share with cigar-specific photo filters, one-directional Pairing (cigar to 3 food + 3 drink), and a Pinterest-style Journal. Taste profile, in-store recommendation, discovery feed, bi-directional pairing, Cigar Doctor, photo import, and vitola identification all move to post-MVP.

MVP-primary persona: Dave the Weekend Ritualist (US, solo smoker, Facebook sharer). All other personas are served post-MVP.

Source of truth: Vitolero PRD v1.0 and Concept Framework v4.0. MVP flows only on this page. Post-MVP roadmap and open questions in separate sub-pages.

Navigation rules · resolved for MVP

Two pips, one boolean. Camera pip (bottom-right) appears on every screen except the camera itself and screens where the camera is the implicit next action (sommelier, cigar card, filter preview, share). Journal pip (top-right) is hidden on the very first session and unlocks the moment the first identification auto-saves. Account creation stays deferred — first Journal tap triggers Google sign-in (PRD §6.3); no upfront wall.

Pattern lineage: Seek (journal-unlocks-after-first-capture), Yuka (anonymous-first), Snapchat (camera-as-home, no chrome). See navigation-plan.md for the full state/affordance matrix and pattern catalog.

Section 1 · MVP

First-time User

Per PRD v1.0 §6.1. Camera is the default state on app open. No sign-up wall. Identification, then share. Pairing is reachable from the share screen or identification result. Journal pip is hidden on the first session — it unlocks the moment the first identification auto-saves. The first tap on the Journal pip triggers Google sign-in (per §6.3).

What changed post April 17: No "Open Scanner" splash — the camera is the home. No manual brand picker — identification surfaces automatically at 80%+ confidence, with up to 3 candidates if ambiguous. No taste-profile hook — the share is the primary CTA after identification. Save is local (account-less) until the user opens Journal.

Section 2 · MVP Hero

Snap & Share

The hero feature. Per PRD v1.0 §4.1, every interaction in the app starts here. Camera default state, photo capture, sommelier-style processing, automatic brand-and-line identification (no vitola), filter selection from 3-5 cigar-specific overlays, magazine-style share card, then handoff to Facebook (deep link via sharer.php) or other platforms (Web Share API).

MVP specs: identification under 10s, 80%+ confidence threshold, up to 3 candidates if ambiguous, brand and line only (vitola deferred — full vitola detection pushed processing to 40-50s). Filter pipeline desaturates the background and keeps the cigar saturated; a semi-transparent strip overlay at the bottom carries brand, line, and a subtle Vitolero mark — magazine-quality, not screenshot-quality. One platform per share. After share, the user leaves the app (post-share reacquisition is acknowledged as an open problem).

Section 3 · MVP

Pairing

Per PRD v1.0 §4.2. Reachable from the share screen or the identification result. Input: a cigar line (from the identification result, or entered manually). Output: exactly 6 items in two groups — 3 food (snack, meal, dessert) and 3 drink (hard liquor or wine, cocktail, coffee or tea). One-directional only: cigar → food/drink. No reverse direction (drink-to-cigar) and no menu-aware pairing in MVP.

MVP specs: 10-15s latency from cigar line input to 6-item result. The list is the final answer — no order, alternatives, or follow-up actions in MVP.

Section 4 · MVP

Journal

Per PRD v1.0 §4.3. A persistent visual gallery of the user's cigar photos. Every cigar identified is auto-saved to the local Journal — no explicit save action. The user can delete entries later. The first tap on Journal triggers Google sign-in to sync across devices. Pinterest-style masonry (or Instagram-style grid — layout choice still open). Each entry shows the filtered photo and a cigar card with product information.

Auto-save model: the cigar enters the Journal the moment identification surfaces. No "save?" prompt — it's already there. Delete is the inverse action, available from the Journal entry detail.
Schema requirement: the Journal data structure must support future recommendations from day one — no rebuild when Phase 2 ships. No recommendation UI in MVP, no recommendation logic in MVP, just a schema that fits both.
Pre-account storage: photos persist locally before sign-in. Local storage is not 100% reliable across devices. The first Journal tap converts that fragile local store into durable cloud storage via Google sign-in.

Section 5 · MVP

Returning User

Per PRD v1.0 §6.2. The app does not open to the camera on every visit — only on a fresh first open. On reopen, the app shows the last photo taken with action buttons available: Share, Pair, Journal, and a button to snap a new cigar. From there the user can re-engage with a previous result, start a fresh capture, or open Journal.

Open problem (PRD §6.3): after the user shares and exits the app, there is currently no mechanism to bring them back. Facebook deep links don't return the user; Web Share API has no callback for image-file shares; PWA push notifications are severely limited on iOS. Snap frequency is the leading indicator to watch. Fabio is investigating technical options. Journal activation creates a more durable hook beyond the next smoke.
