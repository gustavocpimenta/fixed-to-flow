The Problem

Strategy is just the beginning

You have your AI strategy. Maybe you completed AI Activation. Maybe you developed it internally. Either way, you know what you want to do.

Now comes the hard part: making it real. Implementation. Change management. Building internal capability. Maintaining momentum when the initial excitement fades.

Most AI strategies die in the gap between "good idea" and "working system." Teams get stuck. Pilots stall. Champions burn out. Vendors promise quick wins that never materialize.

You need a partner who stays in the trenches with you. Not someone who hands you a plan and leaves.

The Solution

Three phases. As long as you need.

1

Scope Together

We understand your situation and define what you need. Not every engagement includes everything. We scope what makes sense for you: capacity building, implementation sprints, leadership mentoring, or a combination.

2

Execute and Build

Hands on work: implementation sprints where we lead (with partners as needed), capacity building to develop internal AI champions, innovation sessions to explore new opportunities, leadership mentoring to support those leading change.

3

Extend or Complete

When the initial scope is done, you choose: continue with us for more work, or move forward independently. Either way, you have working AI and capable people. You're never locked in.

Questions

Common questions

How long does a typical engagement last?
Depends on scope. Initial interventions might be 3 to 6 months. Some partnerships extend longer. We scope together and adjust as we go. You're never locked in.
Do we need to complete AI Activation first?
No, but you need a clear AI strategy. AI Activation is one way to develop that. If you already have direction, we can start with AI Acceleration directly.
What's the difference between this and hiring consultants?
We're not observers who write reports. We get in the trenches. We lead implementation. We build your capability so you don't need us forever. And we protect human skills while implementing AI.
How much of our team's time does this require?
Depends on scope. Capacity building requires your team's active participation. Implementation sprints need stakeholder availability. We design the engagement around your constraints.
What if we want to change scope mid engagement?
That's expected. We adjust as we learn. If something isn't working, we pivot. If you discover new opportunities, we explore them. The partnership is flexible.
